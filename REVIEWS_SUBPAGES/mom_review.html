Memories of Murder - Full Review

Directed by: Bong Joon-ho

Starring: Song Kang-ho, Kim Sang-kyung, Park Hae-il

Genre: Crime, Drama, Thriller

Released: 2003

This gripping South Korean crime thriller is based on the real-life Hwaseong serial murders, which shook the country in the late 1980s. The film's plot follows two detectives who struggle to find the killer amidst poor forensic technology and lack of evidence. The performances are exceptional, particularly Song Kang-ho’s portrayal of a seasoned, yet frustrated investigator. The film is relentless in its suspense and heartbreaking in its commentary on the futility of the search for justice. It’s a must-watch for fans of crime dramas with strong social commentary.

Rating from CineBox:

9/10

This movie is an intense experience, masterfully crafted with a strong message about the inadequacies of the justice system.
